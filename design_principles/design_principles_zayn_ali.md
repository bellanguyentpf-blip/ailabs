# Design Principles

## Messaging & Clarity

### Answer "What Is This?" and "Is This For Me?" Immediately

Visitors arrive with two core questions. Your page must answer them fast, ideally above the fold.

> "One of the main things that visitors to your website are looking for the first is they're trying to figure out like what is this and I think you answered that pretty clearly in the first line up in the header of your site. The next question that they want to know is is this for me."

> "You just want anybody who's your potential customer to hit your page and instantly just go like oh cool this is exactly the kind of thing that i could use on my website."

**Good Example:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — "It this is a really good headline it's super clear what they actually do."

**Bad Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — "Instant sustainability with automated storytelling... that's a little vague it feels like i am not sure what that says."

---

### Use the Exact Words Your Users Would Use

Go to your existing users, ask them how they'd describe your product, and put those exact words in your headline.

> "One way to test if this is the right thing is to go to talk to your existing users and ask them how would you describe our product and when you do that take the exact words or phrases that they say and just put that right in the headline of your website right at the top because chances are the way that your users think about and describe your product is actually the way that many other people that would be your target users would also think about the benefits and the value of your product."

> "Sometimes as founders we do this where we know all the edge cases of our product and we want to make sure that we come up with you know five words that cover all those edge cases and realistically like it may mean something to you because you have a lot of familiarity with it but people without the context of your product and how it works it doesn't mean anything to them."

**Bad Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — "My hunch is if you were to actually go to your users who have your widget integrated on their site right now and you ask them to describe your product i doubt that they would say ah vernon is like instant sustainability with automated storytelling."

---

### Headlines Should Stand Alone

People skim pages by reading only the headlines. Make sure your headlines tell the full story without requiring paragraph text.

> "A lot of times the way that people will read your page is they're going to scroll down really quickly just kind of skimming and they're just going to read the headlines and so you want to try this yourself for your own product and decide if somebody just read the headlines here would they actually get the information that we would want them to get or is the main information that you want people to take away is it buried in these like small text paragraphs and bullets and screenshots."

**Bad Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — "Right now you don't do a great job through the headlines at least of answering the question what is this and who is it for like is it for me."

---

### Lead with Problems, Not Features

Features tell users what your product does. Problems make them feel why they need it.

> "It seems like you're calling out a lot of kind of like features of things that you can do rather than like benefits or problems that this solves which are generally more attractive to people so it's like you generate custom api responses like cool that's a feature why does somebody care about that and i think that's the question that you want to answer."

> "Some of these features that you call out can totally be turned into you know a problem that people already have so if it's extremely painful to you know generate custom api responses right now and like people have to go through like you know multiple pieces of software or services just to go and do that or do it through terminal and it's really you know it's just a painstaking way of doing things then actually call out you know that pain and then draw me in about how you actually solve that problem."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — Listed features like "modify headers redirect urls switch hosts mock api response" without explaining the problems they solve.

---

### Jargon Can Self-Select (But Test It)

Technical jargon isn't always bad—it can filter for your ideal users. But pressure test it with real users.

> "Obviously there's a lot of jargon in the header here and also in that kind of initial paragraph of text that says what they do i'm not sure that's usually jargon's a bad thing i'm not sure that's a bad thing here because i think what it does is it kind of self selects for the type of people that are most likely to be their users to begin with."

**Context:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — Developer tool where technical terminology may be appropriate for the target audience.

---

### Don't Require Context to Understand the Headline

Your headline should make sense on first read, not after seeing the rest of the page.

> "After reading the paragraph underneath it and seeing the graphic on the side i have a better sense of how the product works and so now i go back and i read that first sentence and i'm like okay i kind of get you know what they were going for here um but it's just not clear like being able to stand on its own."

**Bad Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — The headline "Instant sustainability with automated storytelling" only made sense after reading supporting content.

---

## Visual Design & Screenshots

### Screenshots Need Context to Work

Small screenshots without explanation are useless. Either make them large enough to understand, or explain what's happening.

> "Screenshots are often problematic because you as the founder you're used to looking at your product and you know how everything works and you know everything has a purpose and what that purpose is and it's hard if you're just seeing a screenshot for the first time especially when you don't really have much context on the product overall and even what you do and then to look at this and like orient your head around it is really challenging."

> "This like big yellow circle in the background is definitely like pulling your attention to this screenshot but the screenshot is so small that it's really difficult to see like what it even does that it's almost like not really providing any value."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — "You've got a screenshot where i can't really tell what's going on."

**Good Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — "I think this is actually a really good example of a screenshot working... you're graying out that screenshot and allowing this environmental pledge to really stand out."

---

### Use Macro Screenshots, Not Full App Views

Zooming in on specific features communicates better than showing the entire interface.

> "Unless you want to like zoom in on the screenshot and show me really like the meat of your product... because that's what might actually convince me that your product is solving a problem that i have i would almost like you know either get rid of that or just like get a really good macro screenshot."

**Good Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — Showed a focused screenshot of an e-commerce layout with the environmental pledge overlay.

---

### Video Backgrounds Must Clearly Communicate

Autoplay videos grab attention but can confuse if the content isn't immediately recognizable.

> "Some of the videos are a little confusing like the tennis one makes sense to me and golf does but i see the one that i now understand is personal trainer which i thought was just like a couple people working out and then the far away shot of the ocean visually is really cool but it didn't occur to me that was surfing."

> "I really like video backgrounds but they also can be a little distracting."

**Needs Improvement:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Some video clips required the dropdown menu to understand what sport was being shown.

---

### Avoid Distracting Animations

Animated elements that move while users read create cognitive friction.

> "This sort of like section of i don't know like quotes... it animating is actually a little distracting as well when i was looking at like this content here."

> "The animation is moving a little quickly so it's a little bit hard for me to kind of like you know i have to actually watch it maybe a couple times to see how all of this works."

**Needs Improvement:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Animated quotes section distracted from nearby content.

---

## Trust Building

### Show Real People and Real Reviews

Social proof is essential, especially for startups competing against established companies.

> "I would love to just even see like some of those google reviews here and just seeing like real people who like might have reviewed the service or you know just like have some sort of feedback just to make it feel even more real. Putting that somewhere higher up on the page can really just like increase my confidence to want to actually like book."

> "That's cool too i get to see the faces there's real people behind this there are some customer testimonials there so you've got a lot of like the core elements here."

**Good Example:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Had Google reviews and Decathlon as a partner.

**Good Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — Showed team faces and customer testimonials.

---

### Video Testimonials Beat Text

A quick video of a real customer sharing their experience is more compelling than written quotes.

> "I would really love just to see a video of someone like giving a testimonial like as if they just took the lesson i feel like that would just get me really excited... one of the biggest things that you can almost do on this page is really have one of these people just have a video of themselves really almost like casual talking to the camera and sharing their experience firsthand you know this is a lot of text so you know i think maybe a lot of people might not read all of these but they might be willing to watch a quick you know 10 15 second video of someone talking about their experience."

**Opportunity:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Could benefit from adding video testimonials.

---

### Tell Your Founder Story

As a startup, your personal story and accessibility is a competitive advantage over big companies.

> "As a startup founder you have lots of disadvantages versus uh you know lots of other big companies one of the main advantages that you have is that you are small and you can move quickly and you can give a lot of personalized attention to your customers and so by telling your story and connecting with them on that personal level like hey i was one of you i wanted to learn how to surf here's what i did it was all terrible so that's why i created this product like people will connect with you the founders on the personal level and especially in the early days that's so much of what you're selling to your customers."

> "It's not you know trying to look like some big fancy company it's the fact that there's like real people behind this that if anything were to go wrong or if i were to have a bad experience with my surf lessons that i could reach out to you and you'd probably want to help me like you would care a lot about that."

**Opportunity:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — "Having an about page or more info about your own story can be a really great way to build trust too."

---

### "Trusted By" Logos Matter

Even if users don't recognize the brands, seeing other customers builds confidence.

> "I don't know that i've heard of these um but it's cool that you have some so it tells me like i'm not going to be your first customer so that's good."

**Good Example:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — Showed trusted brands near the top of the page.

---

### "Backed by YC" May Not Resonate with End Users

Investor logos build credibility with some audiences but may not matter to your actual customers.

> "Backed by y commoner might not resonate with like everyone who might be trying to take a surf lesson but decathlon is a pretty big brand."

**Context:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — For consumer products, industry partnerships may be more meaningful than investor backing.

## Layout & Information Architecture

### Reduce Noise Above the Fold

Focus visitors on the single most important action instead of overwhelming them with options.

> "It also strikes me there's just a lot going on here above the fold you've got the headline you've got a lot of different things kind of like a comma separated list in the sub headline there you've got two buttons... you've got also available for all these other browsers you've got five star ratings you've got reviews you've got active users you've got ask a question pop-up thing down there you've got a screenshot where i can't really tell what's going on so anyway there's like a lot going on."

> "The main thing for you to figure out is what are the most important things to put up here and what is the main action that you want to drive all of your users towards and then kind of get rid of all the other stuff maybe it can sit below the fold."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — Cluttered hero section with too many elements competing for attention.

---

### Create Dedicated Landing Pages for Each Service

Targeted landing pages dramatically improve conversion and enable focused marketing.

> "If for no other reason then when you're marketing if you go to like surf communities you may not want to send them just to the home page or they may see golf lessons or something and be like ah this isn't for me um but if you can send them directly to a page about surf lessons i imagine the conversion rate will go way up."

> "Having dedicated pages for all your services is really important especially for seo to acquire customers."

**Good Example:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Created individual pages for each sport type (surf lessons, golf lessons, etc.).

---

### Page Transitions Should Be Obvious

When users click to a new page, they should immediately know they've moved.

> "When i did select surf lessons it felt like i went back to the top of the same page and so i didn't really notice that this entirely changed to be a whole surf page until i scrolled."

**Needs Improvement:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Clicking on a service felt like staying on the same page due to similar hero layout.

---

### Make Scrollability Obvious

Users should immediately know there's more content below the fold.

> "It seems like maybe you're trying to scroll down and it's not letting you is this the entirety of the site... you can totally scroll it doesn't seem obvious when you're up here."

**Needs Improvement:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Full-screen hero made it unclear that more content existed below.

---

### Consolidate Feature Sections

Long pages with repetitive feature sections cause user fatigue.

> "This is also a really big feature section right so you have one two three four five features that we're going over again um and you know you have collaborate with teammates again right so we're just kind of like creating a lot of like redundant info."

> "You have another feature section with six more features so i wonder if you can kind of put maybe some of these sections within you know a different type of feature page instead of just all along like the home page because like aaron was saying this is like extremely long page and i probably would have been fatigued by now."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — Multiple feature sections with redundant information.

---

## Calls to Action

### Pop-ups Need Context

Asking for an email before explaining your value proposition creates friction.

> "Right out of the gate we are hit with a pop-up here save 35 off your first purchase and i don't know what they even do yet but it's trying to hit me up for savings... i'm going to close this so i can figure out what they do and then i'm going to be like great now how do i get that 35 off."

> "It does fatigue users and typically people like click out of them... it's definitely something you can experiment with i think with this it's uh yeah there's a little lack of information here to make a decision and so maybe adding some information about what you guys do or kind of create some like sort of fomo with some messaging here might help."

**Needs Improvement:** [Hokali](#case-study-hokali) — Pop-up appeared before visitors understood the product.

---

### Repeat Your Primary CTA in the Footer

Users who scroll to the bottom are engaged—give them an easy way to convert.

> "You should probably have your main call to action in the footer again too rather than just like join our mailing list or something like that um that's probably not the main thing that you want to drive people towards it's probably like downloading and actually starting to use the product."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — Footer had mailing list signup instead of the main download CTA.

---

### Focus on a Single Primary CTA

Multiple competing actions dilute focus. Optimize for one main conversion path.

> "I would cut away all the noise focus everybody just on ideally a single download link that you want to optimize for."

**Bad Example:** [Requestly](#case-study-requestly) — "You've got two buttons download for chrome download for mac os."

---

### Create Emotional Connection to the Problem

For products tied to meaningful causes, bring that emotional pull to the forefront.

> "When you sort of like want to talk about the problem that you're solving it's so important you know because it's it's about like climate change how do you sort of bring that to the forefront... that kind of has this emotional pull that you almost might be able to like add here."

> "Just like making me feel like i can implement this really really quickly and even almost showing me some of those like causes a little bit more front and center might you know really like lure me in and again like bring that sort of emotional attachment to what you guys are solving for."

**Opportunity:** [Verdin](#case-study-verdin) — Could make environmental impact more emotionally prominent in messaging.

# Case Studies

## Case Study: Hokali

**Website:** Hokali  
**Product:** Online booking platform for sports lessons (surfing, golf, tennis, personal training)

**Overall Assessment:** "Overall i think this is really well done... i think there's a lot of great stuff here they're checking a lot of the boxes of things that you'd want to see on a well done website... my hunches it's probably converting decently well."

### What Worked

- **Clear headline:** "Learning new sports has never been easier book online in three simple steps... it this is a really good headline it's super clear what they actually do"
- **Strong visual design:** "Visually they look really cool it gets my attention i totally get what you do"
- **Dedicated service pages:** "This is cool that you have dedicated pages for each specific type of lesson that you offer"
- **Strong hover states:** "They don't immediately look like they're selectable but uh i think that that hover makes it a little bit more obvious"
- **Social proof elements:** "You have the google reviews here which is great"
- **Partner credibility:** "Decathlon is a pretty big brand"

### What Failed

- **Premature pop-up:** "Right out of the gate we are hit with a pop-up here save 35 off your first purchase and i don't know what they even do yet"
- **Confusing video content:** "Some of the videos are a little confusing like the tennis one makes sense to me and golf does but i see the one that i now understand is personal trainer which i thought was just like a couple people working out and then the far away shot of the ocean visually is really cool but it didn't occur to me that was surfing"
- **Hidden scroll indicator:** "It doesn't seem obvious when you're up here you know at least on like this size of a macbook"
- **Unclear page transitions:** "When i did select surf lessons it felt like i went back to the top of the same page and so i didn't really notice that this entirely changed to be a whole surf page until i scrolled"
- **Distracting animations:** "This sort of like section of i don't know like quotes... it animating is actually a little distracting"
- **YC badge may not resonate:** "Backed by y commoner might not resonate with like everyone who might be trying to take a surf lesson"
- **Text-heavy testimonials:** "This is a lot of text so you know i think maybe a lot of people might not read all of these"

### Key Lessons

- Add context to pop-ups explaining what you do before asking for emails
- Ensure video backgrounds clearly communicate their subject matter
- Make scroll indicators visible on full-screen hero sections
- Create distinct visual changes when navigating between pages
- Reduce or slow down animated elements that compete with content
- Prioritize industry partners over investor logos for consumer products
- Add video testimonials from real customers
- Tell your founder story to build personal connection

## Case Study: Requestly

**Website:** Requestly  
**Product:** Developer tool to intercept and modify HTTP requests (browser extension and desktop app)

### What Worked

- **Usage statistics:** "We've got this many rules created we've got a lot of current users a lot of rules shared so these are all things these are good that you put these in here because it starts to give me confidence that like ah real people are using this"
- **Jargon may self-select:** "I'm not sure that's a bad thing here because i think what it does is it kind of self selects for the type of people that are most likely to be their users to begin with"

### What Failed

- **YC badge prioritized over value prop:** "Backed by y combinator okay cool that they're putting that all the way up at the top i don't know if that's the main thing their users want to see"
- **Cluttered above the fold:** "There's just a lot going on here above the fold you've got the headline you've got a lot of different things kind of like a comma separated list... two buttons... also available for all these other browsers... five star ratings... reviews... active users... ask a question pop-up thing down there... a screenshot where i can't really tell what's going on"
- **Too many competing colors:** "There's just a lot of colors like the blue and the yellow as much as you know like you that seems like your brand colors this like big yellow circle in the background is definitely like pulling your attention to this screenshot"
- **Useless screenshot:** "The screenshot is so small that it's really difficult to see like what it even does that it's almost like not really providing any value"
- **Features instead of benefits:** "It seems like you're calling out a lot of kind of like features of things that you can do rather than like benefits or problems that this solves"
- **Redundant feature sections:** "You have collaborate with teammates again right so we're just kind of like creating a lot of like redundant info and then you also have like this sort of visual diagram of sharing with colleagues"
- **Confusing diagrams:** "That diagram hurts my head trying to figure out what's happening there"
- **Extremely long page:** "This is like extremely long page and i probably would have been fatigued by now"
- **Wrong footer CTA:** "You should probably have your main call to action in the footer again too rather than just like join our mailing list"

### Key Lessons

- Lead with your value proposition, not investor backing
- Identify the single most important action and optimize the entire page for it
- Remove competing elements from above the fold
- Either enlarge screenshots to be readable or remove them entirely
- Reframe features as problems solved
- Test headlines with actual users using their exact words
- Consolidate redundant feature sections
- Remove confusing diagrams that require explanation
- Replace mailing list CTA in footer with primary conversion action

## Case Study: Verdin

**Website:** Verdin  
**Product:** Widget that lets e-commerce brands attach environmental pledges to products and let customers track their impact

### What Worked

- **Clear supporting paragraph:** "Increase customer engagement 10x by attaching environmental pledges to any of your products and services let your customers track their own impact automatically after purchase with updates from world leading providers awesome so that's very clear"
- **Effective screenshot usage:** "I think this is actually a really good example of a screenshot working... you're graying out that screenshot and allowing this environmental pledge to really stand out"
- **Good visual hierarchy:** "Layout typography colors like all the stuff is like seems well done and has good hierarchy to it it's easy to follow it's visually interesting without becoming overwhelming"
- **Trust elements:** "We saw trusted buy up near the top so that's a good way you're trying to build that trust right off the bat"
- **Team visibility:** "I get to see the faces there's real people behind this"
- **Customer testimonials:** "There are some customer testimonials there so you've got a lot of like the core elements here"
- **Partner showcase:** "This section here you know makes me feel like you know i can choose from like some of these really great uh you know partners to implement on my on my website"

### What Failed

- **Vague headline:** "Instant sustainability with automated storytelling... that's a little vague it feels like i am not sure what that says"
- **Internal jargon:** "I'm not sure if like automated storytelling is necessarily like the thing that me as a brand like i might you know i'm trying to solve for i think this might be like sort of like a vision that you have about your product"
- **Headline doesn't stand alone:** "After reading the paragraph underneath it and seeing the graphic on the side i have a better sense of how the product works... but it's just not clear like being able to stand on its own"
- **Fast animation:** "The animation is moving a little quickly so it's a little bit hard for me to kind of like you know i have to actually watch it maybe a couple times to see how all of this works"
- **Unclear context:** "Even with this screenshot on the right i'm not exactly sure where maybe this is occurring if this would be happening on my website or if this is happening on verdin's website"
- **Headlines don't tell the story:** "Right now you don't do a great job through the headlines at least of answering the question what is this and who is it for"

### Key Lessons

- Replace abstract headlines with concrete, literal descriptions
- Ask users how they describe your product and use their exact words
- Ensure headlines alone tell the full story when skimmed
- Slow down animations so users can absorb information
- Make it clear where the product appears (customer's site vs. your platform)
- Bring emotional connection to causes front and center in messaging
- Consider simple framing like "no code solution for instant eco-friendly brand"
- Show environmental partners prominently to create emotional pull
